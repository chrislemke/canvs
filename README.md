## Executive Summary

CANVS is a **persistent, AR-native social layer anchored to physical places**. Instead of consuming life through a decontextualized feed, people experience content where it belongs: at the location it refers to, at the scale it happened, with the atmosphere that gave it meaning.

We are building the infrastructure that turns the planet into an addressable surface. Every bench, alley, beach, mural, cafÃ© table, trailhead, and plaza becomes a node in a living network of memories, knowledge, art, and coordination. A first kiss. A perfect skate trick. A protest that changed someone's life. A conversation that flipped a career. These moments no longer have to end and become invisible. With CANVS, **the place remembers**.

This is now feasible because the enabling stack has matured. World-scale AR anchoring is real and productized. Visual Positioning Systems combine GPS with vision-based localization to place content far more reliably than GPS alone. Persistent, shareable AR at real-world locations is shipping. Spatial computing hardware is moving from demo to platform. CANVS is designed to become **glass-native**: as AR glasses transition into everyday devices, CANVS becomes an always-available layerâ€”less like an app, more like a new default interface for place.

## The Vision: The Internet of Places

We are building the **Internet of Places**.

Today's social platforms are optimized for infinite scrolling, not for human life. They isolate us inside algorithmic loops and replace lived reality with mediated content. CANVS flips the direction of attention.

### Core Principles

**The feed is not the home screen. The world is.**

Content is not something you "go find." It is something you **encounter**, because you are there. Meaning is not manufactured by engagement hacks. Meaning is **in context**.

A story about a sunset is powerful anywhere. A story about a sunset becomes *inevitable* when you are sitting in the exact place it happened, at the same angle, the same horizon line, the same wind.

### The Manifesto

When the world becomes a canvas, CANVS provides the paint.

We reject the premise that human connection must be mediated by rectangles and timelines. We believe that the most meaningful content is not viral content, but contextual content. We believe that presence matters, that geography is not friction but foundation, and that the next great interface is not a screen but the physical world itself.

**CANVS is the social operating layer for spatial computing.**

## The Problem We Solve

**Things happen at places. But digital memory is usually detached from the physical world.**

A first kiss. A perfect skate trick. A protest that changed someone's life. A conversation that flipped a career. A hidden cafÃ© that felt like finding oxygen. Then the moment ends, and the meaning becomes invisible.

### The Status Quo

Someone stands at that place two weeks later and sees... concrete. The emotion is gone. The story exists somewhere in a cloud account, if at all. It is not "there." It is nowhere.
