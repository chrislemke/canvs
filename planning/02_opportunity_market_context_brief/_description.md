# Opportunity / Market Context Brief

## Overview

An Opportunity / Market Context Brief is a strategic document that summarizes the market landscape, business opportunity, and competitive context for a product or initiative. It serves as the business case foundation, helping stakeholders understand why an opportunity is worth pursuing and how the product addresses gaps in the current marketplace. This brief connects market insights to product strategy, providing the context needed for informed decision-making.

## Purpose

- **Justify investment**: Provide evidence that an opportunity is worth pursuing
- **Contextualize the market**: Explain the current state of the market and emerging trends
- **Identify gaps**: Highlight unmet needs and competitive white space
- **Align stakeholders**: Create shared understanding of the business opportunity
- **Inform strategy**: Guide product positioning, prioritization, and go-to-market approach
- **Secure funding**: Build a compelling case for resource allocation

## When to Create

- **Product Inception**: Before committing resources to a new product or major feature
- **Funding Requests**: When seeking approval from leadership or investors
- **Strategic Planning**: During annual or quarterly planning cycles
- **Market Entry**: When evaluating new market segments or geographies
- **Pivot Assessment**: When considering significant changes in product direction
- **Acquisition Evaluation**: When assessing potential acquisitions or partnerships

## Key Components

### 1. Executive Summary
A high-level overview (1-2 paragraphs) putting the opportunity together with direction for execution.

### 2. Market Opportunity
- **Market Size**: TAM/SAM/SOM estimates with methodology
- **Growth Trends**: Market trajectory and key drivers
- **Timing**: Why now is the right time to pursue this opportunity

### 3. Customer Need
- Summary of the problem being solved
- Evidence of demand (research, signals, early validation)
- Customer segments affected

### 4. Competitive Landscape
- Key competitors and their positioning
- Competitive gaps and white space
- Barriers to entry and sustainable advantages

### 5. Product-Market Fit Hypothesis
- How the proposed product addresses the opportunity
- Unique value proposition
- Key differentiators

### 6. Business Viability
- Revenue model and monetization strategy
- Key assumptions and dependencies
- Risk factors and mitigation strategies

### 7. Success Metrics
- How success will be measured
- Key milestones and validation points
- Go/no-go criteria

## Inputs & Dependencies

- Comprehensive market context document (5-15 pages)

- Supporting data appendix
- Risk assessment matrix
- Recommendation with clear next steps
- Input for funding proposals and business cases

## Best Practices

1. **Lead with Insight**: Start with a compelling insight about the market or customer that creates urgency

2. **Be Evidence-Based**: Support all claims with data, research, or validated customer evidence

3. **Quantify the Opportunity**: Use specific numbers for market size, growth rates, and potential revenue

4. **Address Risks Head-On**: Acknowledge risks and explain how they'll be mitigated

5. **Use the 4Ws Framework**: Ensure you cover What, Why, When, and Who clearly

6. **Connect to Strategy**: Show how this opportunity aligns with company-level goals

7. **Keep It Scannable**: Use clear headings, bullets, and visuals for busy stakeholders

## Common Pitfalls

- **Overestimating Market Size**: Using inflated TAM figures without realistic SAM/SOM
- **Ignoring Competition**: Underestimating competitors or claiming "no competition"
- **Confirmation Bias**: Cherry-picking data that supports a predetermined conclusion
- **Missing the "Why Now"**: Failing to explain timing and urgency
- **Vague Differentiation**: Not clearly articulating what makes this opportunity unique
- **Lack of Risk Assessment**: Presenting only the upside without acknowledging challenges
- **Disconnected from Strategy**: Not showing alignment with company priorities

## Examples & References

### Brief Structure Example

> **Executive Summary**: The enterprise knowledge management market is experiencing 23% YoY growth as companies struggle to capture and share institutional knowledge amid remote work. Current solutions are either too complex (enterprise wikis) or too simple (chat archives). Our opportunity is to create an AI-powered knowledge layer that automatically captures, organizes, and surfaces relevant information without requiring behavior change from users.

### Key Questions This Document Should Answer

1. How big is this opportunity?
2. Why is now the right time?
3. What makes us uniquely positioned to win?
4. What are the biggest risks?
5. What does success look like?
