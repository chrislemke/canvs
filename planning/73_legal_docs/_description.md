# Legal Documents

## Overview

Legal Documents for product development encompass all the legal agreements, policies, and disclosures required to operate a product and protect the company and its users. This includes Terms of Service, Privacy Policies, cookie consent, licensing agreements, and compliance documentation. Proper legal documentation is essential for managing risk, meeting regulatory requirements, building user trust, and enabling business operations.

## Purpose

- **Manage risk**: Limit liability and protect the company
- **Ensure compliance**: Meet legal and regulatory requirements
- **Build trust**: Demonstrate transparency to users
- **Enable operations**: Create contractual frameworks
- **Protect users**: Disclose data practices and rights
- **Support sales**: Enable enterprise and regulated customers
- **Avoid disputes**: Set clear expectations and boundaries

## When to Create

- **Product Launch**: Before going live
- **New Features**: When data practices change
- **New Markets**: When entering regulated regions
- **Enterprise Sales**: When customers require agreements
- **Regulatory Changes**: When laws change (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- **Annual Review**: Regular legal review cycle

## Best Practices

1. **Plain Language**: Write for users, not just lawyers.

2. **Layered Approach**: Summary + full legal text.

3. **Keep Updated**: Review when product/law changes.

4. **Version Control**: Track changes over time.

5. **Notify Users**: Communicate significant changes.

6. **Accessibility**: Ensure documents are accessible.

7. **Localization**: Translate for key markets.

8. **Easy Access**: Make documents findable.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Template-Only**: Using templates without customization
- **Outdated**: Not updating as product evolves
- **Inaccessible**: Hard to find or read
- **Over-Broad**: Claiming more rights than needed
- **Missing Disclosures**: Not covering all data practices
- **No Consent**: Missing required consent mechanisms
- **Poor Notice**: Not communicating changes
- **Non-Compliant**: Missing regulatory requirements
