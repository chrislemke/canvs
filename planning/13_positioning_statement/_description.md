# Positioning Statement

## Overview

A Positioning Statement is a concise description of your product and target audience that explains how it fills a specific market need and what makes it different from alternatives. Unlike customer-facing taglines, positioning statements are internal strategic documents that guide all marketing, sales, and product decisions. They ensure consistent messaging and help everyone understand exactly where the product fits in the competitive landscape.

## Purpose

- **Define market position**: Articulate where your product fits in the market
- **Guide messaging**: Provide foundation for all marketing communications
- **Ensure consistency**: Keep all teams aligned on how to describe the product
- **Differentiate**: Clarify what makes your product unique
- **Filter decisions**: Help evaluate whether initiatives support the positioning
- **Enable sales**: Give sales teams a clear way to articulate value

## When to Create

- **Product Launch**: Before going to market with a new product
- **Repositioning**: When changing how the product is perceived
- **Market Entry**: When entering new markets or segments
- **Competitive Response**: When market dynamics shift significantly
- **Brand Refresh**: As part of broader branding initiatives
- **Annual Planning**: Reviewed and updated during strategic planning

## Key Components

### 1. Target Audience
Who is this product for? Define the specific customer segment.

### 2. Market Category
What category or frame of reference does the product compete in?

### 3. Key Benefit
What is the primary value the product delivers?

### 4. Reason to Believe
Why should customers believe you can deliver this benefit?

### 5. Competitive Differentiation
What makes this product different from and better than alternatives?

## Positioning Statement Templates

### Classic Template
> "For [target customer] who [statement of need], [product name] is a [market category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [competitive alternative], our product [statement of differentiation]."

### Extended Template
> "For [target customer] in [market/situation] who need [functional need] and want [emotional need], [product name] is [market category] that delivers [key benefit]. Unlike [alternatives], we [unique differentiator] because [reason to believe]."

### Simplified Format
> "[Product] is the [category] for [audience] that [key differentiation]."

## Product vs. Brand Positioning

| Aspect | Product Positioning | Brand Positioning |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Scope | Individual product | Entire company/brand |
| Focus | Features, benefits | Values, identity |
| Timeframe | Can change per release | More enduring |
| Relationship | Nested under brand | Umbrella for products |

## Best Practices

1. **Keep It Short**: If it's longer than five sentences, you've gone too far. Aim for 1-2 sentences.

2. **Be Specific**: Vague positioning doesn't guide decisions. Include specific target, category, and differentiation.

3. **Test with Customers**: Validate that positioning resonates with actual target customers.

4. **Ground in Reality**: Positioning must reflect actual product capabilities, not aspirations.

5. **Differentiate Meaningfully**: Ensure differentiation is real and valued by customers.

6. **Align All Decisions**: Every marketing and product decision should support the positioning.

7. **Review Regularly**: Markets changeâ€”revisit positioning periodically.

8. **Separate from Taglines**: Positioning statements are internal; taglines are customer-facing.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Too Broad**: Trying to position for everyone, differentiating from no one
- **Feature-Focused**: Listing features instead of communicating value
- **Undifferentiated**: Making claims any competitor could make
- **Aspirational Fiction**: Positioning on capabilities you don't have
- **Category Confusion**: Not being clear about what market you compete in
- **Changing Too Often**: Inconsistent positioning confuses the market
- **Ignoring Competition**: Not acknowledging competitive alternatives
- **Internal Jargon**: Using language customers don't understand

## Examples & References

### Example Positioning Statements

**Slack:**
> "For teams that need to collaborate effectively, Slack is a messaging platform that brings all your communication together. Unlike email, Slack enables real-time, organized conversations that keep everyone aligned."

**Stripe:**
> "For internet businesses that need to accept payments, Stripe is a payment infrastructure that enables any business to accept payments and manage their finances. Unlike legacy payment processors, Stripe is developer-first with modern APIs and comprehensive tools."

**Zoom:**
> "For distributed teams and businesses that need reliable video communication, Zoom is a video conferencing platform that makes virtual meetings effortless. Unlike enterprise solutions, Zoom provides enterprise-grade reliability with consumer-grade simplicity."

### Positioning vs. Tagline

| Positioning Statement (Internal) | Tagline (External) |
|----------------------------------|-------------------|
| Full strategic context | Memorable phrase |
| Target + category + differentiation | Emotional hook |
| Guides decisions | Captures attention |
| 1-2 sentences | 3-7 words |
