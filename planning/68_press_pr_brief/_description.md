# Press / PR Brief

## Overview

A Press/PR Brief is a strategic document that guides external communications for product announcements, company news, or major milestones. It includes press releases, media pitches, key messages, and talking points that ensure consistent, compelling storytelling across all public communications. In 2025, press releases serve not just as media relations tools but also improve search visibility and reinforce brand authority for AI-generated results.

## Purpose

- **Control narrative**: Shape how news is communicated
- **Ensure consistency**: Align all spokespersons on messaging
- **Generate coverage**: Attract media attention
- **Build credibility**: Establish thought leadership
- **Support SEO**: Improve search visibility
- **Document for history**: Create official record
- **Enable distribution**: Prepare for newswire and outreach

## When to Create

- **Product Launches**: New products or major features
- **Company Milestones**: Funding, acquisitions, partnerships
- **Executive Changes**: Leadership announcements
- **Industry Recognition**: Awards, rankings
- **Major Updates**: Significant product enhancements
- **Crisis Response**: When proactive communication needed

## Key Components

### 1. Press Release
- Headline and subheadline
- Dateline
- Lead paragraph (5 Ws)
- Body with details
- Executive quotes
- Boilerplate
- Contact information

### 2. Key Messages
- Primary message (1 sentence)
- Supporting messages (3-5 points)
- Proof points and data
- Differentiation

### 3. Talking Points
- Anticipated questions
- Approved responses
- Topics to avoid
- Bridge phrases

### 4. Media Strategy
- Target outlets
- Journalist list
- Pitch angles
- Embargo details

### 5. Supporting Materials
- Product images
- Logos and brand assets
- Executive headshots
- Fact sheet

## Press Release Format

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

[Headline: Clear, Concise, Under 80 Characters]
[Subheadline: Additional Context]

[CITY, STATE] – [Date] – [Lead paragraph answering Who, What, When,
Where, Why, and How in 25-30 words]

[Body paragraph 1: Expand on the news, provide context]

[Body paragraph 2: Include supporting data, features, benefits]

"[Quote from executive providing insight, vision, or significance],"
said [Name, Title, Company].

[Body paragraph 3: Additional details, availability, pricing]

[Optional second quote from partner or customer]

[Call to action: How to learn more, sign up, or participate]

About [Company]
[Boilerplate: 2-3 sentences about the company, mission, key stats]

Media Contact:
[Name]
[Email]
[Phone]

###
```

## Press Release Best Practices

### Headlines
- Clear and captivating
- Under 80 characters
- Include key message
- Jargon-free

### Lead Paragraph
- Answer the 5 Ws
- Most important info first
- 25-35 words maximum
- Hook the reader

### Quotes
- Sound like a real person
- Add insight, not repetition
- Include emotion or vision
- Keep under 30 words

### Content
- 300-500 words total
- Focus on "why it matters"
- Include multimedia (88% of PR pros do)
- Add relevant keywords for SEO

## Best Practices

1. **Lead with News Value**: Focus on why this matters to readers.

2. **Include Multimedia**: Images and videos increase engagement.

3. **Make Quotes Meaningful**: Don't waste space on generic statements.

4. **Target Relevant Media**: Pitch journalists who cover your space.

5. **Time Strategically**: Avoid major news days, consider time zones.

6. **Prepare Spokespeople**: Brief executives on key messages.

7. **Optimize for Search**: Include relevant keywords naturally.

8. **Follow Up**: Personalized outreach after distribution.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Jargon Overload**: Using industry speak readers don't understand
- **Burying the News**: Most important info not in first paragraph
- **Boring Quotes**: Generic statements that add nothing
- **Too Long**: Releases over 500 words
- **No Visuals**: Missing images or multimedia
- **Wrong Timing**: Releasing during major news events
- **No Follow-Up**: Sending and forgetting
- **Legal Issues**: Unverified claims or compliance problems

## Media Outreach Tips

### Finding Journalists
- Research bylines in target publications
- Use media databases (Muck Rack, Cision)
- Follow relevant journalists on social media
- Build relationships before pitches

### Pitch Email Format
- Subject: Concise, specific, newsworthy
- Opening: Why this matters to their readers
- Body: Key facts in 2-3 sentences
- Close: Offer interview or assets
- Keep under 200 words
