Manifesto

The end of the feed.
The beginning of the world.

Today’s platforms isolate us inside algorithmic loops. CANVS flips attention outward: the world becomes the home screen.

The problem

Context loss & the “ghost event”

Things happen at places—but digital memory gets detached. Weeks later, you return and the meaning is invisible.

The solution

Persistent emotional anchoring

Place becomes the index. Content stays where it belongs—aligned to the geometry of the world.

Core experience

A spatial browser for the real world.

One mental model, three modes. AR is the view. Map is the minimap. Time is the scrub.

The loop

Look → Understand → Act

Micro‑encounters: see a story, feel something, respond or add context, move on—carrying the place with you.

Anchor Runtime

Positioning abstraction as product leverage

    AI mediation

    LLM agents as a “reality filter”

    Not a chatbot. A layer that summarizes a place, prevents overwhelm, and makes relevance feel like taste.

    Distribution

    Web‑native sharing

    “Open in browser” becomes a growth lever: location‑locked experiences shared via a link—not an install.

    Trust, safety & governance

    The layer must be safe by design.

    A spatial social layer amplifies both beauty and risk. Precision is a privilege, not a default.

    Non‑negotiables

    Anti‑stalking posture

      Spatial moderation

      Rules that understand place

      Harmful content isn’t just “a post” — it’s attached to a location. Moderation needs geofenced rules and contextual integrity.

      Private space shield

      Strong exclusion zones and owner claims prevent nuisance mechanics and real‑world conflict.

      Strategic roadmap

      2026–2030: from phone‑first to spatial OS.

      Build the core loop, scale the meaning graph, transition to glass, then platformize.
