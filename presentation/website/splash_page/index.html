The Problem

We walk through a world full of invisible meaning

"Things happen at places. But digital memory is detached from the physical world."

A first kiss. A perfect skate trick. A protest that changed someone's life. A conversation that flipped a career. A hidden café that felt like finding oxygen.

Then the moment ends—and the meaning becomes invisible. Someone stands at that place two weeks later and sees… concrete. The emotion is gone.

It's not "there." It's nowhere.

The feed is not the home screen. The world is.

Content is not something you "go find." It's something you encounter, because you are there.

Meaning is not manufactured by engagement hacks. Meaning is in context.

The Solution

Persistent Emotional Anchoring

CANVS makes moments spatially persistent. Not as a gimmick, but as a new medium: Place becomes the index.

Reality Time Capsules

Content—text, audio, video, 3D captures, sketches—pinned to real-world locations so future visitors can "open" the moment where it happened.

Asynchronous Presence

Experience a moment later, not by "watching a video," but by seeing the memory in situ, aligned to the geometry of the world.

World-Locked Anchoring

Modern AR stacks combine GPS with vision-based localization for stable, multi-tier anchoring that persists across time and devices.

The Experience

The Spatial Browser

A browser that lets you "read" places the way you read websites. Three modes, one mental model.

01

AR Mode

"Magic Window"

Look at the world and see location-anchored content where it belongs. Stories float exactly where they happened.

02

Map Mode

"Spatial Radar"

See nearby layers, hotspots, and trails. The map is not the product—it's the minimap for the real world.

03

Time Mode

"Temporal Scrub"

Slide through time to see what happened here: yesterday, last summer, 10 years ago. Places remember.

Building Blocks

Spatial Primitives

The fundamental objects that make up the meaning layer. Each one anchored to the world.

Pins

Simple anchors: notes, photos, audio, links, tips, warnings, micro-stories.

"Best espresso in the city—trust me."

Bubbles

Shared containers anchored to places—group memories, collective stories.

"We used to meet here every Friday."

Time Capsules

Rich artifacts: multi-media + 3D capture + timeline. Walkable memory fragments.

"Open in 5 years, at this exact spot."

Trails

A sequence of anchors forming a narrative route—curated walks, emotional journeys.

"The city through immigrant food."

Drops

Geo-fenced releases: music, artwork, collectibles that only unlock at a location and time.

"Available here, tonight only."

Portals

Places anchored to other places via story logic—connections across geography.

"This spot is the sibling of that spot."
Use Cases

Re-enchanting Reality

Emotional Layer

Shared memories, personal stories, moments that matter.

The Skating Bubble

Friends leave a group bubble at the rink. Years later, someone returns alone and opens it—hearing laughter and seeing spatial clips aligned to the ice.

Sunset Stories

A global atlas of romance: "Sit here at 19:42. Perfect angle." The world becomes a map of beautiful moments.

Celebration Spots

Mark your favorite places for birthdays, anniversaries, and milestones. Return each year and see how the memories grow.

Letters to Future Me

A capsule you can only open when you physically return. Time + place as the key.

Utility Layer

Hyper-local knowledge, practical wisdom, community intelligence.

Hidden Menu Layer

Recommendations pinned to a specific table or counter: "Order it this way." Local secrets, openly shared.

Cafe Secrets

Discover the best seat for working, the quietest corner for reading, or which barista makes the perfect latte. Local knowledge, shared.

Place Stories

Restaurants and venues share their history, today's specials, or stories behind the menu—pinned right where you're standing.

Trail Conditions

Real-time hiking intel anchored to trail segments: "Muddy after the bridge" or "Best wildflowers at this bend in May."

Creative Layer

Urban culture, play, art that lives where it belongs.

Street Art Threads

Discussions float in front of murals—timelines, artist credits, community response—without touching the wall.

Location Drops

Exclusive content appears when a place "activates" at a time. Artists release where their art lives.

Invisible Galleries

AR art installations that exist only in specific locations. Turn any corner into a canvas, any park into a sculpture garden.

Sound Walks

Location-triggered audio experiences: ambient soundscapes, narrated histories, or musical compositions that unfold as you move.
